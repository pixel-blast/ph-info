PH Water Check

Introduction

Clean water and proper sanitation are important for a healthy life. Everyone should have access to safe water to drink, cook, and stay clean. Without it, people can get sick and face many problems. SDG 6 works to make sure clean water and good sanitation are available for all.

Features of PH

  • PH measures acidity or alkalinity on a 0 - 14 scale.
  • The PH value of drinking water is between 6.5 - 8.5.
  • PH affects aquatic life and human health.

About PH Value

  • Safe Drinking pH: 6.5 - 8.5 (WHO standard)
  • Pure Water pH: 7 (neutral)
  • Sanitation Range: 6 - 9
  • Low pH (<6.5): Can cause pipe corrosion and metal contamination
  • High pH (>8.5): May taste bitter and reduce disinfectant power

Advantages of PH Value

  • Environmental:
  • Supports healthy aquatic life
  • Maintains soil quality
  • Ensures clean water
  • Health:
  • Prevents waterborne diseases
  • Protects skin and eyes
  • Aids digestion

Disadvantages of PH Value

  • Environmental:
  • Pollutes water and harms aquatic life
  • Damages soil quality
  • Disrupts ecosystems
  • Health:
  • Causes skin and eye irritation
  • Leads to digestive problems
  • Increases risk of waterborne diseases

Water Slogans

  • "Every drop counts!"
  • "Save water, save life!"
  • "Water is life, conserve it!"
  • "Save water today, secure tomorrow!"

Conclusion

pH levels are key to water quality, environmental health, and human well-being. Managing pH helps prevent pollution, protect infrastructure, and support sustainability.
